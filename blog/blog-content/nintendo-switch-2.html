A Long Time Coming

The Nintendo Switch 2 has been one of the most anticipated console releases in years. After the original Switch launched back in 2017, Nintendo kept it going far longer than most people expected — and now, nearly a decade later, the sequel is finally here.

What I Like

The new magnetic Joy-Con system is a massive upgrade. No more rail-sliding that wore out over time — the magnets feel secure and the click is satisfying. Also, it seems that these controllers are a bit more stick-drift proof, meaning they last longer than before. The larger screen is also a welcome change, especially for handheld play. And if handheld doesn't work out for you, there's always the new mouse-style play.

The performance bump is noticeable too. Games load faster and run smoother, which makes a big difference coming from the original hardware.

What I'm Not Sure About

The price is steep. Both the console itself and the new games are more expensive than I'd like, especially for a younger audience. It feels like Nintendo is pushing toward a more premium market.

Also, the fact that the original Switch is somewhat ignored now is a bit sad. and I mean that not only are they slowly stopping the creation of Switch 1 games, but the new chat function is something I would love to have in the original.

Final Verdict

Overall, the Switch 2 is a solid upgrade — not a revolution, but a meaningful evolution. If you're still on the original Switch, it's worth considering, but know that you'll only have so much content to play with. If you're new to Nintendo, this is a great time to jump in.
