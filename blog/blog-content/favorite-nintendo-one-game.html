Loads of Em'

There are a LOT of Switch games that have meen published ove the years. Not only that, Nintendo franchise has practically never stopped developing. Here are a couple of my favorite Switch games.

#10 - Fortnite

Fortnite, launched in 2017, has had its wins and losses. It gains and loses popularity randomly. Even so, it's really fun, but only if you're playing with friends. Otherwise, it's a bit boring. As for the other creator content, again, it's really only fun if you have other friends. (It's fun to hear your friends crash out 😜) Otherwise, the overall gameplay is fun and the quality of the game is high.

#9 - Fall Guys

Another game by Epic Studios, Fall Guys never really got popular, but it's really fun. Unlike Fortnite, it's still fun to play solo. The occasional seasonal maps are also fun, and if you're the creative type, level building is available. A problem I have with the game is that it repeats maps over and over, which is great for beginner skill building, but otherwise, it's really repetitive.

#8 - Minecraft

An original and highly popular, Minecraft is a game that anyone can enjoy. The gameplay quality is good, but I prefer Java Edition, so that's a personal disadvantage. Also, the game may still be popular, but it has lost its original glamour and fame. The good thing is, there's always something you can do in Minecraft, but a problem is Nintendo Online- which, in my opinion, is overpriced and blocks a lot of fun. For example, my personally favorite server, Cubecraft.

#7 - Super Mario Wonder

Your typical Mario game, remastered in the 2020. This one really brings back the old Mario vibes, mixed with new elements, like the bubble, elephant, and drill. A great game that just never ends!

#6 - Mario + Rabbids Kingdom Battle

Fusing heroes and comedic rabbits, Mario + Rabbids takes apon a journey where Mario and gang, accompanied by Rabbid counterparts, must save the world (that part sounds familiar...). It's an overall really fun game with lots of mind-bending puzzles and combat, with a great storyline to go with it. I would say this game would be more popular if the sequel, Mario + Rabbids Sparks of Hope were better.

#5 - Paper Mario

Now, by this, I mean the whole series of Paper Mario. All of the games are golden, incorparating the motion controls and a unique battle style, along with 3D platforming. A great choice for anyone!

#4 - Pokemon: Legends Z-A

What seems like the last Pokémon game (other than Pokémon Champions) is a great return to Mega Evolution and Kalos overall. The DLC not only adds extra content, but boosts the overall game enjoyment as well One of the best Pokémon games, in my opinion!

#3 - Super Smash Bros. Ultimate

A game that has passed through many Nintendo consoles, Smash Bros. brings both the head-to-head combat that is enjoyable whether you're with friends or not, and Adventure Mode, a new addition that is really enjoyable. In my opinion, a main benefit to this game is that it doesn't end. Sure, Adventure Mode has a climax, but you can start fighting again even if you finish, along with the undying satisfaction of crushing your friends!

#2 - Mario Kart 8 Deluxe

Is there really anything to say? It's Mario. Kart. I don't think I need to give any further elaboration.

#1 - Super Mario Odyessy

A Switch user has to know about this game- and if they do, they have to play this game. It is impossible to not. A great game, great plotline, great quaility. The new focus on Cappy is a great addition to the story. Fun, funny, with a mix of 8-bit nostalgia, there really shouldn't be a Switch without this game.
